## Clothes source mapping

All torso garments in this mod only render their collar/shoulder region within the 600×600
portrait canvas, so visual comparison was done on collar style/shape plus garment semantics
(name, mod category). Each garment is copied in the four size variants
(`s/m/l/xl`) as `<profession>-<size>.png`; the table lists the `-m-` file, the other
sizes are its siblings in the same folder. Size `s` is worn by children.
Sizes are gender-specific for adults (mod's `Requirements.cs` PawnBodyType rules):
adult female Thin/Standard wear `m` and Fat/Hulk wear `l`; adult male
Thin/Standard/Hulk wear `l` and Fat/Hulk wear `xl` — the selection engine mirrors
this in `CLOTHES_SIZE_BY_GENDER_BUILD`.

### Age stages

Head/face/neck/hair sources are stage-prefixed: heads carry the full
`c/t/y/m/e` mod tiers; faces and necks use art stages `c/t/a` (adult art
covers young/middle/elder); hair uses `c/a`. Face trait picks and hair names
are identical across stages, so a character's identity persists as they age.
Clothes are age-agnostic (`s` is worn by children per the mod's PawnBodyType
rules); beards are adult-only.

## Notes on curation

- **Face layers are split into `face-inner/` and `face-outer/`.**
  InnerFace and OuterFace are not visually identical: InnerFace is the
  colored skin-tone/detail layer (eyes, lips, skin coloring) and
  OuterFace is a black line-art overlay (eyebrow/eye/nose/mouth
  outlines). Both layers are required to render a complete face;
  downstream tasks should composite `face-inner` (tinted with the
  character's skin tone) underneath `face-outer` (no tint).
- Clothes were picked from across all bundled mod packs (Vanilla,
  Vanilla Expanded Apparel/Armour/Medieval/Settlers), using the
  `-m` (medium) build size, matching each profession to the closest
  visual garment. See `assets/portraits/source/clothes/` for the
  profession → garment mapping.
